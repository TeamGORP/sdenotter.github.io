\r\n This guideline addresses the issue of investments in an entity or group of\r\n related entities by property and casualty (P&C) insurance companies\r\n and sets out the Office\'s policy with respect to limits on these\r\n investments. This guideline applies to all federally regulated P&C\r\n insurance companies.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n The Office is concerned about the concentration of investments in an\r\n entity or a group of related entities in considering the diversification\r\n needs of a prudent portfolio policy.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n The Office believes that large exposures need to be subject to limits,\r\n notwithstanding the quality of individual investments.\r\n

\r\n Investment\r\n Concentration Limit\r\n

\r\n

\r\n All property and casualty insurance companies must set out in writing, as\r\n part of their overall prudent portfolio investment policy, internal\r\n policies on investment concentration. P&C insurance companies must\r\n also have in place management information and control systems necessary to\r\n give effect to their written policies.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Notwithstanding the limit addressed in this guideline, it is expected that\r\n companies will establish lower limits and their investments will approach\r\n the guideline limit on an exceptional basis.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Canadian Company\r\n

\r\n

\r\n The aggregate book value of investments by a Canadian P&C insurance\r\n company in any entity or group of related entities shall\r\n not exceed 5 per cent of the company\'s assets.

\r\n

Investments\r\n include investments made by all subsidiary companies (see example in\r\n Appendix).\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Canadian Branch of a Foreign Company\r\n

\r\n

\r\n The aggregate book value of investments of a foreign company in any entity\r\n or group of related entities vested in trust shall not\r\n exceed 5 per cent of the company\'s assets in Canada.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Excess Investments\r\n

\r\n

\r\n A company with excess investments in an entity or in a group of related\r\n entities must reduce its exposure below the 5 per cent limit. Meanwhile,\r\n the amount of investments in excess of the 5 per cent limit will be\r\n deducted from the total assets in calculating the company\'s Assets\r\n Adequacy Test.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Definitions\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Assets\r\n

\r\n

\r\n For a Canadian company, the total book value of assets reported on the\r\n balance sheet of the regulatory return (P&C-1).\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Assets\r\n in Canada\r\n

\r\n

\r\n For a foreign company, the total book value of assets under the control of\r\n the Minister (vested in trust in Canada) as reported on the balance sheet\r\n of the regulatory return (P&C-2).\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Investments\r\n

\r\n

\r\n An investment within the normal meaning of the term as used by P&C\r\n insurance companies. P&C companies should also take into consideration other forms of\r\n exposure to an entity or group of related entities even in cases where an\r\n investment or commitment is not shown on the balance sheet; for example,\r\n options, futures, forward contracts and unfunded portions of committed\r\n loans.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n An investment excludes loans to, and loans guaranteed or securities issued\r\n or guaranteed by the Government of Canada, a Canadian province or an OECD\r\n central government.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Entity\r\n

\r\n

\r\n As defined in the Insurance Companies Act, a natural person, a\r\n body corporate, trust, partnership, fund, unincorporated association or\r\n organization, an agency of the Crown in right of Canada or of a province,\r\n and any agency of a foreign government.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Related\r\n Entity\r\n

\r\n

\r\n An entity is related to another entity in those circumstances in which a\r\n person would be related to a company pursuant to subsection 518(1) of the\r\n Act.\r\n
