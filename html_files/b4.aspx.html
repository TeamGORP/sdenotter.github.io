\r\n This guideline sets out prudential considerations relating to the lending\r\n of securities by federally regulated banks, authorized foreign banks in\r\n respect of their business in Canada (foreign bank branches - FBBs), trust\r\n and loan companies and cooperative credit associations ("financial\r\n institutions").\r\n

\r\n
\r\n

\r\n Introduction\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Traditionally, security loans have been short term transactions designed\r\n to assist the liquidity of securities markets by enabling dealers/brokers\r\n to cover failed deliveries or short sales. However, some security loans\r\n may also have been undertaken for longer terms in which liquidity is a\r\n secondary consideration. Financial institutions should be fully cognizant\r\n of the additional risks stemming from such longer term transactions and\r\n should ensure that they have the systems and controls in place to identify\r\n and control these risks.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Financial institutions should ensure that securities lending activities\r\n are conducted in a safe and prudent manner and they should seek\r\n appropriate professional advice to ensure that controls and procedures are\r\n comprehensive and sound.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Collateral\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Financial institutions should at all times hold adequate collateral to\r\n protect themselves against the risks associated with securities lending.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n The amount of collateral taken for securities lending should reflect best\r\n practices in local markets. In Canada, the current market practice is to\r\n obtain collateral of at least 102Footnote 1 per cent of the market value of the securities lent. Management is\r\n expected to ensure that the margin of collateral in excess of market value\r\n of securities lent is appropriate at all times. This margin should provide\r\n adequate protection against volatility and liquidity problems that may\r\n arise for securities lent and for securities held as collateral. Both\r\n loaned and collateral securities should be "marked to market" at least\r\n daily. Shortfalls in the amount of collateral should be rectified\r\n immediately.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n A financial institution\'s policy on acceptable collateral should be\r\n consistent with the financial institution\'s overall investment policies as\r\n established by the board of directors. Accordingly, financial institutions\r\n should only take securities as collateral that are acceptable as a direct\r\n investment.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n For securities lending within North America, eligible collateral should be\r\n readily marketable and would normally be restricted to the following\r\n assets, denominated in Canadian or U.S. dollars:\r\n

\r\n
    \r\n
  • \r\n

    \r\n cash;\r\n

    \r\n
    • \r\n
  • \r\n

    \r\n widely-traded debt instruments having a rating of single A (or the\r\n equivalent) or higher from a recognized, widely followed North\r\n American credit rating agency;\r\n

    \r\n
    • \r\n
  • \r\n

    \r\n commercial paper rated A-1 or R-1 or the equivalent by a recognized,\r\n widely followed North American credit rating agency;\r\n

    \r\n
    • \r\n
  • \r\n

    \r\n acceptances of banks and trust and loan companies whose short-term\r\n deposits are rated A-1 or R-1 or the equivalent by a recognized,\r\n widely followed North American credit rating agency; and\r\n

    \r\n
    • \r\n
  • \r\n

    \r\n high quality common and preferred shares. \r\n

    \r\n
    • \r\n
\r\n

\r\n Eligible collateral also includes:\r\n

\r\n
    \r\n
  • \r\n

    \r\n unconditional, irrevocable letters of credit that comply with the\r\n standards of the International Chamber of Commerce and which are\r\n issued by banks and trust and loan companies whose short-term deposits\r\n are rated A-1 or R-1 or the equivalent by a recognized, widely\r\n followed North American credit rating agency; and\r\n

    \r\n
    • \r\n
  • \r\n

    \r\n unconditional and irrevocable guarantees of banks and trust and loan\r\n companies whose short-term deposits are rated A-1 or R-1 or the\r\n equivalent by a recognized, widely followed North American credit\r\n rating agency.\r\n

    \r\n
    • \r\n
\r\n

\r\n Convertible preferred shares and convertible debt instruments may be taken\r\n as collateral when they are immediately convertible into the underlying\r\n security lent.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Securities lending activities in Organization for Economic Co-operation\r\n and Development (OECD) countries other than Canada and the U.S. should be\r\n in accordance with the above criteria, but financial institutions may also\r\n accept collateral denominated in the currency of the country in which the\r\n lending occurs, and debt instruments issued by that country\'s central\r\n government.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Controls\r\n and Records\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Financial institutions should ensure that they have appropriate internal\r\n controls, procedures and records in place. Controls should include a list\r\n of approved borrowers, consistent with the financial institution\'s lending\r\n policies that are based on generally accepted credit worthiness standards\r\n with specified lending limits for each borrower. The list should be:\r\n

\r\n
    \r\n
  • \r\n

    \r\n available at all times to the personnel responsible for administering\r\n the securities lending program; and\r\n

    \r\n
    • \r\n
  • \r\n

    \r\n reviewed regularly by appropriate senior management.\r\n

    \r\n
    • \r\n
\r\n

\r\n The financial institution\'s board of directors (or a committee designated\r\n by the board and reporting to the board)Footnote 2 \r\n should, at least annually, review and approve:\r\n

\r\n
    \r\n
  • \r\n

    \r\n the general credit worthiness standards used in establishing the list\r\n of approved borrowers which should be consistent with the financial\r\n institution\'s overall lending policies as established by the board;\r\n and\r\n

    \r\n
    • \r\n
  • \r\n

    \r\n overall and individual securities lending limits. These limits should\r\n take into account other exposures established by the board.\r\n

    \r\n
    • \r\n
\r\n

\r\n External or internal auditors should verify at appropriate intervals:\r\n

\r\n
    \r\n
  • \r\n

    \r\n that the list of borrowers conforms to the criteria established by the\r\n board (or a committee designated by the board and reporting to the\r\n board);\r\n

    \r\n
    • \r\n
  • \r\n

    \r\n that securities accepted as collateral are consistent with the overall\r\n investment policies established by the board of directors; and\r\n

    \r\n
    • \r\n
  • \r\n

    \r\n the existence of securities lent and taken as collateral.\r\n

    \r\n
    • \r\n
\r\n

\r\n A designated senior manager of the financial institution should receive\r\n regular, comprehensive and timely reports summarizing the financial\r\n institution\'s securities lending activities to allow the manager to judge\r\n whether the activities are being properly administered.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Use of\r\n an Agent\r\n

\r\n

\r\n A financial institution may employ an agent for the administration of its\r\n securities lending program. The financial institution\'s board of directors\r\n (or a committee designated by the board and reporting to the board) should\r\n approve the choice of an agent. The agent selected should be an\r\n institution that can demonstrate an ability to perform the required duties\r\n in a competent and responsible manner. Administrative and reporting\r\n arrangements satisfactory to the financial institution should be clearly\r\n set out and agreed to in writing.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Other\r\n Considerations\r\n

\r\n

\r\n A financial institution should enter into a legally binding master\r\n agreement with each borrower establishing the basis for all security loans\r\n between the two parties. When an agent is involved, there should be a\r\n master agreement between the financial institution and agent as well as a\r\n master agreement between borrower and agent. The agreement(s) should set\r\n out the rights and obligations of all parties including the right to\r\n immediate set-off for the financial institution (or agent acting for the\r\n financial institution) in the event of the borrower\'s failure to return\r\n the securities as specified in contractual arrangements.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Financial institutions should ensure that they are in compliance with\r\n applicable Protection of Assets\r\n Regulations.\r\n
