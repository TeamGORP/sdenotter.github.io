\r\n

This bulletin outlines safekeeping requirements for assets of \r\n Canadian and foreign insurers under the Insurance Companies \r\n Act (the Act), including the use of foreign depositories. For \r\n Canadian companies, the authority on the use of foreign depositories \r\n rests in the Protection of Assets (Insurance Companies) Regulations, \r\n made pursuant to Section 703(g) of the Act. Safekeeping requirements \r\n for foreign companies are set out in Section 611 of the Act.

\r\n

Canadian Companies

\r\n

The Protection of Assets (Insurance Companies) Regulations \r\n (the Regulations) set out the safekeeping requirements for \r\n Canadian companies. Subsection 6 prescribes safekeeping requirements \r\n for assets:

\r\n
\r\n

6

\r\n
\r\n
\r\n

(1) Subject to subsection (2) and section 7, a company shall \r\n ensure that every security is kept securely, in a manner that \r\n prevents unauthorized access to the security, in the custody of: \r\n

\r\n
    \r\n
  1. the company; or
    2. \r\n
  2. an entity that is authorized to act as a custodian of securities \r\n or as a depository or clearing agency for securities by a law \r\n of the jurisdiction in which the entity is carrying on business. \r\n
    3. \r\n
\r\n

(2) Subsection (1) does not apply in respect of a security that \r\n is:

\r\n
    \r\n
  1. under the control of the government of a jurisdiction in \r\n which the company is carrying on business;
    2. \r\n
  2. pledged as collateral for the indebtedness or potential indebtedness \r\n of the company;
    3. \r\n
  3. loaned to a person pursuant to a written agreement; or d) \r\n in transit.
    4. \r\n
\r\n
\r\n
\r\n

Section 7 further stipulates that the company must enter into \r\n a written custodial agreement before placing its assets in safekeeping \r\n with a duly authorized custodian, depository or clearing house for \r\n securities:

\r\n
\r\n

7

\r\n
\r\n
\r\n

A company shall not place a security in the custody of an entity \r\n referred to in paragraph 6(1)(b) unless the company has entered \r\n into a written custodial agreement with that entity.

\r\n
\r\n
\r\n

A Canadian insurance company may maintain securities in a foreign \r\n depository as long as the company has a written custodial agreement \r\n with the foreign depository.

\r\n

A Canadian insurance company may maintain securities in a foreign \r\n depository through its custodian as long as the Canadian company \r\n has a written custodial agreement with its custodian, which in turn \r\n has a written agreement with the depository.

\r\n

Section 8 of the Regulations prescribes safekeeping requirements \r\n for net proceeds of securities transactions:

\r\n
\r\n

8

\r\n
\r\n
\r\n

A company shall, on a daily basis, deposit any net amount received \r\n by the company as a result of any security transaction in an account \r\n kept by the company:

\r\n
    \r\n
  1. in the company;
    2. \r\n
  2. with a financial institution that is authorized to accept \r\n deposits by a law of the jurisdiction where the financial institution \r\n is carrying on business;
    3. \r\n
  3. with a trust company that is authorized to hold money in \r\n trust by a law of the jurisdiction where the trust company carries \r\n on business;
    4. \r\n
  4. with the government of the jurisdiction in which the company \r\n is carrying on business, or with an agency thereof that is authorized \r\n to act as a custodian of securities; or
    5. \r\n
  5. with The Canadian Depository for Securities Limited (CDS). \r\n
    6. \r\n
\r\n
\r\n
\r\n

A company cannot keep cash proceeds of a securities transaction \r\n in a foreign depository. As long as the company maintains an account \r\n as described above and receives any net amount in that account on \r\n a daily basis, the requirements of section 8 would be satisfied. \r\n

\r\n

Foreign Companies

\r\n

Foreign companies are subject to Part XIII of the Insurance \r\n Companies Act. By virtue of their structure, foreign companies \r\n are subject to a different regulatory regime than Canadian companies. \r\n

\r\n

Subsection 611(1) in Part XIII of the Act prescribes that:

\r\n
\r\n

611

\r\n
\r\n
\r\n

(1) The assets that a foreign company is required to maintain \r\n in Canada pursuant to sections 608 and 609 and the regulations \r\n made pursuant to section 610 shall be vested in trust in a Canadian \r\n financial institution chosen by the foreign company and approved \r\n by the Superintendent.

\r\n
\r\n
\r\n

Subsection 2(1) of the Act defines "assets in Canada" as assets \r\n vested in trust for a foreign company under Part XIII of the Act. \r\n

\r\n

Subsection 611(3) of the Act requires that:

\r\n
\r\n

611

\r\n
\r\n
\r\n

A trust deed must be approved by the Superintendent before it \r\n is entered into.

\r\n
\r\n
\r\n

Section 10 of OSFI\'s Standard Form Trust Agreement permits the \r\n trustee to deposit, subject to the written approval of the Superintendent, \r\n any of the assets vested in trust with the CDS. The Trust Agreement \r\n does not permit the trustee to vest in trust assets with foreign \r\n depositories. The Superintendent does not approve trust agreements \r\n that differ materially from the Standard Form Trust Agreement.

\r\n

OSFI maintains a prohibition on the use of foreign depositories \r\n by foreign companies for prudential reasons. \r\n OSFI requires legal certainty that it has undisputed and timely \r\n access to assets held in depositories when required. OSFI must be \r\n able to repatriate and realize upon those assets without having \r\n to face legal and other challenges which may conspire to delay or \r\n reduce the amounts ultimately available to Canadian policyholders. \r\n

\r\n
