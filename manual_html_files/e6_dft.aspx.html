\r\n I. Statement of Regulatory Principles\r\n

\r\n
  1. \r\n

    This Guideline builds upon the following:\r\n

    \r\n

    \r\n Self-dealing regime: The objective of the legislated self-dealing\r\n regime is to preclude transactions that could inappropriately benefit\r\n persons with influence over a federally regulated financial institutionFootnote 1 (FRFI). Concerns fall into two\r\n categories:\r\n

    \r\n
      \r\n
    1. \r\n

      \r\n that certain transactions result in owners and other related partiesFootnote 2 drawing inappropriate benefits that\r\n could have a detrimental impact on the solvency of an FRFI, and\r\n

      \r\n
      2. \r\n
    2. \r\n

      \r\n the perception that owners and other related parties drew\r\n inappropriate benefits from certain transactions because of their\r\n position of influence.\r\n

      \r\n
      3. \r\n
    \r\n

    \r\n Consequently, subject to a number of specific exceptions in the FRFI\r\n legislation and regulations, FRFIs cannot directly or indirectly enter\r\n into a transaction with a related partyFootnote 3.\r\n (For example, the FRFI legislation specifically permits an FRFI to make a\r\n loan to a related party if the loan is fully secured by securities of or\r\n guaranteed by the Government of Canada or the government of a provinceFootnote 4. Because the legislation specifically\r\n permits this transaction, this Guideline has no application in regard to\r\n this transaction.)\r\n

    \r\n

    \r\n This Guideline deals with the exceptions to this general prohibition on\r\n related party transactions when measured by criteria that have been\r\n established by an FRFI\xe2\x80\x99s Conduct Review Committee (CRC) and approved in\r\n writing by the SuperintendentFootnote 5.\r\n

    \r\n

    \r\n Given that nominal or immaterial transactions are an exception to the\r\n general prohibition on related party transactions, the criteria set out in\r\n this Guideline should be interpreted restrictively.\r\n

    \r\n
\r\n

\r\n Application of the Guideline\r\n

\r\n

  1. \r\n This Guideline establishes criteria that the Superintendent considers\r\n appropriate for FRFIs to use in determining whether a transaction with a\r\n related party is nominal or immaterial. If the FRFI\xe2\x80\x99s CRC has established\r\n criteria at least as stringent as those set out in the Table of\r\n Nominal/Immaterial Value Transactions in Part III of this Guideline,\r\n these criteria will be deemed to have been approved by the Superintendent.\r\n

    2. \r\n

  2. \r\n If an FRFI\xe2\x80\x99s CRC would like to establish other materiality criteria,\r\n OSFI will assess these individually. OSFI will require supporting\r\n information as described in the OSFI\xe2\x80\x99s Transaction Instruction on Related\r\n Party Transactions of Nominal Value.

    3. \r\n
  3. \r\n

    \r\n This Guideline also applies to the exception that permits FRFIs to\r\n enter into certain transactions with a \xe2\x80\x9cnarrow group\xe2\x80\x9d of related parties\r\n that includes a director or senior officer of the FRFI or a spouse,\r\n common-law partner or child who is less than eighteen years of age of the\r\n director or senior officer or any entity controlled by a director or\r\n senior officer of the FRFI or a spouse, common-law partner or child who is\r\n less than eighteen years of age of the director or senior officer provided\r\n that the person or the entity is a related party only\r\n because of that connectionFootnote 6. The\r\n legislation requires board approval to exceed legislated aggregate\r\n exposure limits to this \xe2\x80\x9cnarrow group\xe2\x80\x9dFootnote 7;\r\n however, the legislation does not require nominal or immaterial\r\n transactions to be included in the calculation of the aggregate exposure\r\n to the \xe2\x80\x9cnarrow group\xe2\x80\x9d when measured by criteria established by the FRFI\xe2\x80\x99s\r\n CRC and approved in writing by the SuperintendentFootnote 8.\r\n In this context, transactions that are nominal or immaterial according to\r\n criteria established by the FRFI\xe2\x80\x99s CRC in accordance with this Guideline\r\n will be excluded from the aggregate exposure to the \xe2\x80\x9cnarrow group\xe2\x80\x9d\r\n calculation.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n II. Criteria\r\n

\r\n

  1. \r\n OSFI believes that virtually any act or agreement in which more than\r\n one person is concerned, and through which relations between the persons\r\n are altered, is a transaction. OSFI considers a series of payments related\r\n to the same commitment to be one transaction, and not separate\r\n transactions. For example, the acquisition or disposition of a $10 million\r\n asset that is spread over 20 payments of $500,000 constitutes a $10\r\n million transaction. OSFI does not subscribe to the view that these are 20\r\n individual transactions for the purposes of this Guideline. Further, OSFI\r\n believes that all transactions should be valued on a "gross" basis. OSFI\r\n considers it inappropriate for an FRFI to measure a transaction against\r\n materiality criteria based on the transaction\'s "net" impact. Accordingly,\r\n for example, the sale of a $10 million building to a related party for\r\n consideration of another building valued at $9.9 million and cash of\r\n $100,000 constitutes a $10 million transaction. OSFI does not subscribe to\r\n the view that these transactions have no net impact on the FRFI and hence,\r\n are immaterial to it.\r\n

    2. \r\n

  2. \r\n For the purposes of the Table of Nominal/Immaterial Value\r\n Transactions in Part III of this Guideline:\r\n

    \r\n
      \r\n
    1. \r\n \xe2\x80\x9cRegulatory capital\xe2\x80\x9d is the financial year opening balance of\r\n \xe2\x80\x9cregulatory capital\xe2\x80\x9d as defined in the Regulatory Capital\r\n Regulations.\r\n
      2. \r\n
    2. \r\n \xe2\x80\x9cSecurity\xe2\x80\x9d means a security as defined in section 2 of the FRFI\r\n legislation and subsection 488(4) of the BA, subsection 476(4) of the\r\n TLCA, subsection 520(4) of the ICA and subsection 412(4) of\r\n the CCAA.\r\n
      3. \r\n
    3. \r\n \xe2\x80\x9cLoan\xe2\x80\x9d has the same meaning as defined in subsection 488(3) of the BA,\r\n subsection 476(3) of the TLCA, subsection 520(3) of the ICA\r\n and subsection 412(3) of the CCAA.\r\n
      4. \r\n
    4. \r\n \xe2\x80\x9cSecurities of a related party\xe2\x80\x9d refers to securities issued by a\r\n related party.\r\n
      5. \r\n
    \r\n
    3. \r\n

  3. \r\n The Table of Nominal/Immaterial Value Transactions in Part III\r\n of this Guideline divides related party transactions into 6 categories\r\n based on the nature of the transaction and the degree of associated risk.\r\n

    4. \r\n

  4. \r\n Transactions at or under the "transaction threshold" are nominal or\r\n immaterial and are not included in calculating the "aggregate threshold".\r\n

    5. \r\n

  5. \r\n Transactions over the "transaction threshold" are nominal or immaterial\r\n until the total value of all such transactions exceeds the "aggregate\r\n threshold".\r\n

    6. \r\n

  6. \r\n No transaction over the \xe2\x80\x9ctransaction threshold\xe2\x80\x9d may be entered into\r\n that would cause the \xe2\x80\x9caggregate threshold\xe2\x80\x9d to be exceeded.\r\n

    7. \r\n

  7. \r\n In assessing whether the value of a transaction is immaterial or\r\n nominal to the FRFI, the FRFI must assess the value of the transaction as\r\n if it had been carried out on market terms and conditions.\r\n

\r\n

\r\n III. Table of Nominal/Immaterial Value Transactions\r\n

\r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n
\r\n Category\r\n \r\n Transaction Threshold\r\n \r\n Aggregate Threshold\r\n
\r\n 1. The purchase or provision of services from or to related parties.\r\n \r\n Greater of $50,000 or 1/50 of 1% of regulatory capital.\r\n \r\n Greater of $200,000 or 1/4 of 1% of regulatory capital per related\r\n party, per financial year.\r\n
\r\n 2. Leasing to or from related parties.\r\n \r\n Greater of $50,000 or 1/50 of 1% of regulatory capital.\r\n \r\n Greater of $200,000 or 1/4 of 1% of regulatory capital per related\r\n party, per financial year.\r\n
\r\n 3. The purchase or sale of assets, other than Securities, from or to\r\n a related party.\r\n \r\n Greater of $100,000 or 1/10 of 1% of regulatory capital.\r\n \r\n Greater of $250,000 or 1/2 of 1% of regulatory capital per related\r\n party, per financial year.\r\n
\r\n 4. Where the related party transaction consists of:\r\n
    \r\n
  1. \r\n the acquisition of Securities of a related party;\r\n
    2. \r\n
  2. \r\n the acquisition of Securities of a third party from a related\r\n party;\r\n
    3. \r\n
  3. \r\n the disposition of Securities to a related party; or
    4. \r\n
  4. any of\r\n the following transactions with an entity:\r\n
      \r\n
    1. \r\n a Loan to the related party;\r\n
      2. \r\n
    2. \r\n an endorsement or other guarantee of an obligation of the\r\n related party; or\r\n
      3. \r\n
    3. \r\n taking an assignment or otherwise acquiring a Loan to such a\r\n related party.\r\n
      4. \r\n
    \r\n
    5. \r\n
\r\n 		\r\n Greater of $250,000 or 1/10 of 1% of regulatory capital.\r\n \r\n Greater of $750,000 or 1% of regulatory capital per related party\r\n per financial year.\r\n
\r\n 5. Providing funding or credit to a related party who is a natural\r\n person, other than a full-time senior officer, by way of\r\n
    \r\n
  1. \r\n a Loan, endorsement or other guarantee;\r\n
    2. \r\n
  2. \r\n taking an assignment or otherwise acquiring a Loan to such\r\n related party.\r\n
    3. \r\n
\r\n 		\r\n $250,000\r\n \r\n Greater of $500,000 or 1/50th of 1% of regulatory capital per\r\n related party per financial year.\r\n
\r\n 6. Transactions not referenced in, or specifically excluded from, a\r\n criteria as referenced above.\r\n \r\n Greater of $50,000 or 1/50 of 1% of regulatory capital.\r\n \r\n Greater of $50,000 or 1/25 of 1% of regulatory capital per related\r\n party, per financial year.\r\n
