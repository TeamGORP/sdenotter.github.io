\r\n

Reinsurance is an important risk management tool available to \r\n an insurer. It can be used to reduce insurance risks and the volatility \r\n of financial results, stabilize solvency, make more efficient use \r\n of capital, better withstand catastrophic events, increase underwriting \r\n capacity, and to draw on reinsurers\xe2\x80\x99 expertise. However, reinsurance \r\n exposes an insurer to other risks, including operational, legal, \r\n counterparty, and liquidity risks. The combination of these risks \r\n can make reinsurance complex and challenging to implement effectively. \r\n Inadequate reinsurance risk management practices and procedures \r\n can materially affect an insurer\xe2\x80\x99s financial soundness and reputation, \r\n and can ultimately contribute to its failure.

\r\n

This Guideline sets out OSFI\xe2\x80\x99s expectations for effective reinsurance \r\n practices and procedures. These practices and procedures should \r\n form an important part of an insurer\xe2\x80\x99s overall reinsurance risk \r\n management policy. It applies to all federally \r\n regulated insurers, including life insurers and property and casualty \r\n insurers, domestic insurance companies and foreign insurance companies \r\n in respect of their insurance business in Canada, registered reinsurers \r\n Footnote 1, \r\n and fraternal benefit societies (collectively referred to as FRIs), \r\n that are party to reinsurance cessions, retrocessions, and, where \r\n applicable, to assumption reinsurance transactionsFootnote 2. \r\n

\r\n

FRIs are reminded that OSFI\xe2\x80\x99s Guideline on Corporate Governance \r\n extends to the business of reinsurance with respect to ensuring \r\n effective oversight and risk management policies and procedures. \r\n

Key Principles

\r\n

The following reinsurance principles are intended to assist FRIs \r\n in developing prudent approaches to managing their reinsurance risks. \r\n OSFI will assess a FRI\xe2\x80\x99s reinsurance risk management policy against \r\n these principles and, where considered necessary, will require remedial \r\n action consistent with its early intervention mandate.

\r\n
\r\n

1. A FRI should have a sound and comprehensive reinsurance \r\n risk management policy, subject to the oversight of the FRI\xe2\x80\x99s \r\n Board of Directors and implementation by senior managementFootnote 3.

\r\n
\r\n

Reinsurance Risk Management Policy (RRMP)

\r\n

OSFI expects that a FRI\xe2\x80\x99s RRMP will form an integral component \r\n of its overall enterprise-wide risk management plan.

\r\n

The RRMP should reflect the scale, nature and complexity of a \r\n FRI\xe2\x80\x99s business, and have regard for its risk appetiteFootnote 4 and risk toleranceFootnote 5. \r\n OSFI expects the RRMP to document the significant elements of the \r\n FRI\xe2\x80\x99s approach to managing risks through reinsurance, including \r\n the purpose and objectives for seeking reinsurance, risk diversification \r\n objectives, risk concentration limitsFootnote 6, \r\n ceding limitsFootnote 7, \r\n and the practices and procedures for managing and controlling its \r\n reinsurance risks.

\r\n

The RRMP should detail:

\r\n
    \r\n
  • the roles and responsibilities for those positions charged with \r\n implementing the RRMP;
    • \r\n
  • the process for ensuring that the RRMP is updated to reflect \r\n changing market conditions;and,
    • \r\n
  • the FRI\xe2\x80\x99s policy on the use of registered and unregistered reinsurance.
    • \r\n
\r\n

Additionally, a FRI must assess the adequacy and effectiveness \r\n of the reinsurance arrangements to ensure that exposures to large \r\n and catastrophic losses are adequately addressed. This may require \r\n stress testing of exceptional but plausible scenarios to determine \r\n if the reinsurance arrangements entered into are adequate at mitigating \r\n losses to acceptable levels in accordance with the FRI\xe2\x80\x99s risk appetite \r\n and risk tolerance.

\r\n

Board of Directors (Board) and Senior Management Oversight

\r\n

The RRMP should be overseen by the BoardFootnote 8 \r\n and implemented by senior management. At a minimum, a FRI\xe2\x80\x99s Board \r\n should review and approve the FRI\xe2\x80\x99s RRMP as part of its annual review \r\n of the enterprise-wide risk management plan. Senior management has \r\n the responsibility of ensuring that the RRMP is operationalized \r\n through the dedication of adequate resources and is implemented \r\n by the risk management and business line officers and managers charged \r\n with the day-to-day responsibility for the RRMP.

\r\n

\xc2\xa0

\r\n

It is the responsibility of the Board and senior management to \r\n ensure that appropriate policies, procedures, and internal controls \r\n exist to monitor the effectiveness of, and operational compliance \r\n with, the RRMP on an on-going basis.

\r\n
\r\n

2. A FRI should perform a sufficient level of due diligence \r\n on its reinsurance counterparties on an on-going basis to ensure \r\n that the FRI is aware of its counterparty risk and is able to \r\n assess and manage such risk.

\r\n
\r\n

A FRI should evaluate the ability of all current and prospective \r\n reinsurance counterparties to meet their liabilities under exceptional \r\n but plausible adverse events on an on-going basis. The level of \r\n a FRI\xe2\x80\x99s due diligence on any reinsurance counterparty should be \r\n commensurate with its level of exposure to that counterparty. Footnote 9 \r\n

\r\n

In the evaluation of its current and prospective reinsurance counterparties, \r\n however, a FRI should generally not rely solely on third parties, \r\n including rating agency assessmentsFootnote 10 \r\n or broker analysis and recommendations.Footnote 11 \r\n Prudent practice dictates that the FRI should, to an extent proportional \r\n to the importance of such counterparty, conduct its own due diligence \r\n on the financial strength and capabilities of all reinsurance counterparties. \r\n

\r\n

When performing its due diligence, a FRI should give consideration \r\n to, among other things, the reinsurance counterparty\xe2\x80\x99s:

\r\n
    \r\n
  • claims payment record;
    • \r\n
  • expected future claims obligations;
    • \r\n
  • balance sheet strength;funding sources, including its level \r\n of and access to capital, and form, amount and sources of liquidity; \r\n
    • \r\n
  • management, including the quality of its governance practices \r\n and procedures; and,
    • \r\n
  • retrocession arrangements and the direct or indirect impact \r\n they may have on the FRI\xe2\x80\x99s own arrangements with the reinsurer. \r\n Footnote 12
    • \r\n
\r\n

Similarly, a FRI that is a reinsurer should, commensurate with \r\n its level of exposure to the cedant, conduct its own due diligence \r\n on the risk management and risk assessment criteria of the cedant. \r\n

\r\n

The evaluation of each of a FRI\xe2\x80\x99s reinsurance counterparties should \r\n be updated throughout the life of the reinsurance contract. In cases \r\n where there may be material exposures to incurred but not reported \r\n losses, management should ensure that the evaluation continues beyond \r\n the expiration date of the contract to ensure that the FRI assesses \r\n potential reinsurance recoverables from expected future claims. \r\n

\r\n

OSFI expects a higher level of due diligence by a FRI in respect \r\n of any current or prospective reinsurance arrangement with an unregistered \r\n reinsurerFootnote 13 or with a \r\n cedant that is not regulated by OSFI. This assessment of counterparty \r\n risk should consider the regulatory and supervisory regime, and \r\n the legal and insolvency frameworks of the unregistered party\xe2\x80\x99s \r\n home jurisdiction. Footnote 14

\r\n
\r\n

3. The terms and conditions of the reinsurance contract \r\n should provide clarity and certainty on reinsurance coverage. \r\n

\r\n
\r\n

A FRI should have processes and procedures in place to ensure \r\n that a comprehensive, written, and bindingFootnote 15 \r\n reinsurance contract is executed prior to the effective date of \r\n reinsurance coverage. To achieve clarity and certainty on reinsurance \r\n coverage, a reinsurance contract should be unambiguous, and there \r\n should be complete and final agreement of all material terms and \r\n conditions of the contract, documented in writing, by all parties \r\n prior to the contract\xe2\x80\x99s effective date.

\r\n

OSFI recognizes that there may be situations where a comprehensive \r\n reinsurance contract is only duly executed by all parties after \r\n the effective date. In such circumstances, historical practice has \r\n been that the reinsurance coverage during this interim period is \r\n usually set out in a less formal document (e.g., slip, cover note, \r\n letter of proposal, binding letter of intent, hereinafter referred \r\n to as the \xe2\x80\x9csummary document\xe2\x80\x9d). If an event were to occur within \r\n this interim period, lack of certainty relating to the terms and \r\n conditions of the reinsurance coverage in the summary document could \r\n result in actual operational and reputational risks for both the \r\n cedant and the reinsurer. In an effort to mitigate these risks, \r\n OSFI expects FRIs to:

\r\n
    \r\n
  1. obtain contractually binding summary documents prior to the \r\n effective date of the reinsurance coverage, including, but not \r\n limited to electronic copies, or original hard copies, of signed \r\n documents that set out: \r\n
      \r\n
    • the premium / consideration paid by the cedant;
      • \r\n
    • the percentage of risk assumed by each reinsurer;
      • \r\n
    • the risk(s) reinsured;
      • \r\n
    • the duration of the coverage;
      • \r\n
    • where applicable, any exclusions to terms of coverage; and,
      • \r\n
    • any standard clauses that are to be relied upon or incorporated \r\n by reference into the reinsurance contract;
      • \r\n
    \r\n
    2. \r\n
  2. address, within the summary document, any material issues most \r\n likely to arise, including all variable or unique agreement terms; \r\n and,
    3. \r\n
  3. ensure that all final comprehensive reinsurance contracts, including \r\n any amendments thereto, bear the duly authorized signature of \r\n both the ceding company and the reinsurer(s)Footnote 16 \r\n within a relatively short timeframe having regard for the nature, \r\n complexity and materiality of the agreement (e.g., within 120 \r\n days of execution).
    4. \r\n
\r\n

Generally, a reinsurance contract should stand on its own, providing \r\n the necessary clarity and legal certainty on reinsurance coverage. \r\n OSFI acknowledges, however, that there may be situations where it \r\n is necessary and appropriate for a FRI to enter into a supplemental \r\n or subordinated reinsurance contract, a side letter, or other types \r\n of arrangements that are ancillary to, and form part of, the main \r\n reinsurance contract. In addition to ensuring that these arrangements \r\n meet the requirements of this Guideline, the FRI should be transparent \r\n with stakeholders about these arrangements, ensure that such amendments \r\n are appropriately reflected in its financial statements, and ensure \r\n that they do not adversely change the terms or conditions of the \r\n original contract to the detriment of policyholders.

\r\n
\r\n

4. A ceding FRI should not be adversely affected by the \r\n terms and conditions of a reinsurance contract.

\r\n
\r\n

The terms and conditions of a binding reinsurance agreement should \r\n provide that funds will be available to cover policyholderFootnote 17 claims in the event of either the cedant\xe2\x80\x99s \r\n or reinsurer\xe2\x80\x99s insolvency. To this end, reinsurance contracts should \r\n include an \xe2\x80\x9cinsolvency clause\xe2\x80\x9d, and particular attention should \r\n be paid to the appropriate useFootnote 18 \r\n of \xe2\x80\x9coff-set\xe2\x80\x9d or \xe2\x80\x9ccut-through\xe2\x80\x9d clauses, the structure of \xe2\x80\x9cfunds withheld\xe2\x80\x9d \r\n arrangementsFootnote 19 , and other \r\n such types of terms or conditions that may frustrate the scheme \r\n of priorities under the Winding-Up and Restructuring Act (WURA). \r\n

\r\n

Ceding FRIs should ensure that all reinsurance contracts contain \r\n an insolvency clause clarifying that the reinsurer must continue \r\n to make full payments to an insolvent cedant without any reduction \r\n resulting solely from the cedant\xe2\x80\x99s insolvency. Such a clause provides \r\n greater certainty that reinsurance receivables remain within the \r\n overall general estate of the insolvent ceding company, or as part \r\n of the assets in Canada of a foreign insurance company as defined \r\n under the WURA and the Insurance Companies Act (ICA), rather \r\n than being allocated toward the payment of specific claims of creditors \r\n or policyholders.

\r\n

Reinsurance contracts should not contain other types of terms \r\n or conditions that may limit a troubled or insolvent cedant\xe2\x80\x99s \r\n ability to enforce the contractual obligations of a reinsurer, or \r\n that may adversely affect the treatment of any claims in respect \r\n of the cedant\xe2\x80\x99s policyholders. and cut-through clauses may allow \r\n certain creditors or policyholders to have preferential Off-set \r\n treatment over other claims, contrary to the scheme of distribution \r\n in the WURA. In the case of off-set clauses, for example, where \r\n the ceding company is a foreign insurance company authorized to \r\n insure in Canada risks, the reinsurer should not have any right \r\n of off-set against the obligations of the ceding company other than \r\n those related to the ceding company\xe2\x80\x99s insurance business in Canada. \r\n

\r\n

If a reinsurance contract provides for a funds withheld arrangement, \r\n the contract must clearly provide that, in the event of the cedant\xe2\x80\x99s \r\n or reinsuer\xe2\x80\x99s insolvency, the funds withheld, less any surplus due \r\n back to the reinsurer, must form part of the property of the cedant\xe2\x80\x99s \r\n general estate, or part of the assets in Canada of a foreign insurance \r\n company as defined under the WURA and the ICA.

\r\n

OSFI expects all contracts related to reinsurance coverage to \r\n stipulate a choice of forum, a choice of law, and the appointment \r\n of agents for service of legal processes that are necessary to ensure \r\n the reinsurance contract and any disputes arising from such contract \r\n are subject to the laws and courts of a Canadian province or another \r\n legal jurisdiction of, in the reasonable opinion of the FRI, equivalent \r\n or greater reliability and certainty which has a natural connection \r\n to the transaction.

\r\n

Guideline Administration

\r\n

Supervisory Information

\r\n

Enhanced transparency will allow OSFI to better understand the \r\n economic impacts and risks associated with a FRI\xe2\x80\x99s reinsurance arrangements. \r\n A FRI is required to maintain and provide to OSFI, upon request, \r\n its RRMP and a complete description of all its reinsurance arrangements, \r\n including levels of reinsurance, the due diligence performed on \r\n reinsurance counterparties, and the proportion of registered and \r\n unregistered cessions.

\r\n

A FRI should promptly inform OSFI if it becomes aware of any reinsurance \r\n issues that could materially impact its financial condition.

\r\n

Capital / Asset Requirements

\r\n

Reinsurance may be a major part of a FRI\xe2\x80\x99s overall risk management \r\n program. Non-adherence to the principles in this Guideline could \r\n result in a reinsurance program or reinsurance contract that does \r\n not provide the intended protection to the ceding company. It may \r\n also compromise the availability of balances that may have been \r\n otherwise recoverable. If a FRI fails to meet the principles set \r\n out in this Guideline, on a case-by-case basis, OSFI may not grant \r\n a capital / asset credit for the reinsurance arrangement, or may, \r\n commensurate with risk, use its discretionary authority under the \r\n ICA, to adjust the FRI\xe2\x80\x99s capital / asset requirements or target \r\n solvency ratios to compensate for reinsurance that is not, or may \r\n not, be wholly effective or reliable.

\r\n

Federally approved provincial / territorial reinsurers may lose \r\n their status if they fail to meet the expectations set out in this \r\n Guideline.

\r\n

Reinsurance Declaration

\r\n

A senior officer of a FRI should make an annual reinsurance declaration \r\n to the Board or Chief Agent, as appropriate, to confirm that the \r\n FRI\xe2\x80\x99s reinsurance risk management practices and procedures meet, \r\n except as otherwise disclosed in such declaration, the standards \r\n set in this Guideline. The declaration should include an attestation \r\n that the FRI\xe2\x80\x99s reinsurance arrangements effect a risk transfer and \r\n that they are accounted for in the appropriate manner.

\r\n

When a deviation from this Guideline has taken place over such \r\n year, the nature and extent of the deviation, and the measures taken \r\n or proposed to correct or otherwise mitigate the risk associated \r\n with such deviation, should be documented and disclosed to the Board \r\n and to OSFI in full.

\r\n

Implementation / Transition

\r\n

OSFI encourages each FRI to implement the principles and expectations \r\n set out in this Guideline as soon as practically possible.

\r\n

OSFI expects that each FRI will comply with Principle 1, including \r\n securing Board approval of its RRMP, by July 1, 2011.Footnote 20Once \r\n the RRMP has been approved, each FRI is expected to implement and \r\n fully comply with the remaining principles and expectations of this \r\n Guideline, including the filing of its first reinsurance declaration \r\n to the Board, by July 1, 2012. Footnote 21

\r\n

With respect to existing multi-year contracts that do not naturally \r\n come up for renewal during the transition period, OSFI expects that \r\n each FRI will take all commercially reasonable efforts during the \r\n transition period to make these contracts B-3 compliant. The designated \r\n OSFI Relationship Manager will follow each FRI\xe2\x80\x99s efforts and progress. \r\n

\r\n

Other OSFI Guidance

\r\n

This Guideline is complementary to, and should be read in conjunction \r\n with, other OSFI guidance that implicitly or explicitly addresses \r\n various elements of reinsurance or governance, including:

\r\n
    \r\n
  • Guidance for Reinsurance Security \r\n Agreements, which sets out OSFI\xe2\x80\x99s minimum standards for \r\n reinsurance security agreements;
    • \r\n
  • Guideline B-10 \xe2\x80\x93 Outsourcing of Business \r\n Activities, Functions and Processes, if a FRI uses the \r\n services of a reinsurance intermediary to facilitate its reinsurance \r\n RRMP;
    • \r\n
  • Guideline A \xe2\x80\x93 Minimum Continuing \r\n Capital and Surplus Requirements for federally regulated \r\n life insurers and societies, which includes the Test of Adequacy \r\n of Assets and Margin Requirements for foreign life insurance companies; \r\n \r\n Minimum Capital Test for federally regulated domestic \r\n property and casualty insurers; and (Guideline A-2) Branch Adequacy of Assets Test for \r\n foreign property and casualty companies;
    • \r\n
  • Corporate Governance Guideline, \r\n which provides information to Boards and management of FRIs about \r\n OSFI\xe2\x80\x99s expectations on corporate governance; and,
    • \r\n
  • Guideline E-17: Background Checks \r\n on Directors and Senior Management of Federally Regulated Entities \r\n (FRE), which outlines principles to assess the suitability \r\n and integrity of directors and senior management by FREs.
    • \r\n
\r\n

A FRI should consider that, before reinsuring with an unregistered \r\n related party, an approval may be required under subsection 523(2) \r\n or 597(1) of the ICA. A ceding FRI must seek the requisite approval \r\n under subsection 254(2), 254(2.01) or 587.1(2) of the ICA prior \r\n to causing itself to be reinsured on an assumption basis.

Absent extenuating \r\n circumstances (e.g., a troubled FRI), such an arrangement could \r\n weaken the underwriting standards and discipline at the ceding \r\n FRI. A FRI may, however, occasionally cede a portion, or even \r\n 100 per cent, of a specific line of business or a particular \r\n type of risk that is ancillary to its core business to, for \r\n example, underwrite the business for purely marketing or relationship \r\n purposes. Cessions of existing lines of business at 100 per \r\n cent may, depending on the circumstances, also be acceptable \r\n to OSFI.
